Better than Earth

WORLD

  • Prelude to Martian Emigration

    Beset by climate catastrophe and geopolitical unrest, Earth's inhabitants unite to forge the "First Expedition," a monumental spacecraft designed for Mars colonization. This vessel symbolizes humanity's technological pinnacle and its desperate quest for a fresh start beyond a dying Earth. It marks the dawn of an audacious journey towards Mars, promising a new chapter in human endurance and innovation.

  • Unexpected
    Challenges

    As the first wave of colonists embarked on their voyage to Mars, their dreams of a new beginning were soon met with unforeseen obstacles. The ambitious project to terraform the planet encountered mysterious energies—elusive forces that defied understanding and interfered with their efforts, challenging their vision of a rejuvenated Earth. Furthermore, the harsh Martian landscape proved hostile to Earth-made technology, leading to critical equipment failures and testing the resilience of these pioneering settlers.

  • Emergence
    of Bitrium

    Leveraging the remnants of the First Expedition, the settlers established a thriving colony on Mars, initially buoyed by Earth's support which gradually waned. Amidst growing independence, they uncovered a vast, near-infinite energy source—Bitrium—mastering its power to fuel their new world.

  • New Settlers
    of Mars

    A specialized group, known as the Phobos and Senatus, was formed to oversee the potent Martian resource, Bitrium. Their ambition to share it with Earth was thwarted when Bitrium dissipated outside Mars, ultimately transforming into a protective virtual atmosphere enveloping the colony.

  • Mellow,
    ‘Better than Earth’

    The revelation of Mars' advancements spurred a mass exodus from Earth, centering life around the iconic Mars Tower. United by hope for a crisis-free future, humanity collaboratively strengthens Mars, persistently exploring its vastness for growth. Facing new challenges, this burgeoning society aspires to eclipse Earth, continuously forging ahead.
